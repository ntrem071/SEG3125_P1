Before You Book

To ensure a safe and compliant experience at our studio, we require all clients, and parents or legal guardians of minors aged 10-15, to present a physical government-issued ID at their appointment. Parents or guardians must also be present to sign necessary legal documents on the day of the appointment.

Accepted Forms of ID:
  • Passport
  • Driver's License
  • Birth Certificate* (with supporting photo ID)
  • Ontario Photo ID Card
  • Status Card
  • Nexus
  • Firearm License

*Note: Birth certificates alone are insufficient. They must be accompanied by a photo ID.

Health cards are not accepted as primary identification but can be used to supplement another valid ID.

Important: Piercings will not be performed without acceptable identification. Failure to bring proper ID to your appointment may result in the full price of the piercing being charged and the appointment being marked as a ‘no-show’.

We do not pierce individuals under the age of 10. Additionally, clients are asked not to bring children under 10 to accompany them to their appointments. For minors aged 10-15, we will perform no more than two piercings in a single session.

Age-Specific Piercing Guidelines:
  • 10+: Single Lobe, Double Lobe
  • 13+: Helix (including Forward Helix), Flat, Tragus, Rook, Daith, Conch
  • 14+: Nostril, Septum, Navel
  • 16+: All piercings except nipple and genital
  • 18+: All piercings

For clients aged 10-15, a parent or legal guardian must accompany them to their appointment. Both the minor and their guardian must present valid government-issued identification.

Please note that extended family members or close friends cannot act as legal guardians for the purpose of the appointment, regardless of their relationship or residency status. If you arrive without the proper legal guardian, we will be unable to proceed with the piercing and you may be charged the full cost of the appointment as a ‘no show’.

Important: If you are a legal guardian through adoption or have been granted custody of the minor, you must provide proof of guardianship at the appointment.

We accept various payment methods for your convenience:

  • Most major credit cards, including American Express
  • Interac
  • Cash

While we accept credit gift cards in theory, our payment terminal may have difficulty processing them reliably. Therefore, we advise against relying solely on credit gift cards for payment.

Please note that we are unable to split payments between multiple cards. However, you can split payment between a card and cash if needed.

Aftercare

Your piercer may tailor aftercare instructions to suit your individual piercing, body, and healing process. Follow their recommendations for best results.

Cleaning Instructions

We generally advise cleaning your piercing twice a day. Here's how:

  1. Prepare: Wash your hands thoroughly and set up in a clean area.
  2. Sterile Saline Solution: Spray both the front and back of your piercing with a sterile saline solution. This should be the only product used for cleaning, aside from water.
  3. Rinse: Dampen a clean piece of paper towel with warm water and use it to gently rinse off the saline solution, removing any buildup or crusties.
  4. Dry: Use a new piece of clean, dry paper towel or a hair dryer on a low cool setting to completely dry the area. Be gentle to avoid pushing the jewelry in and out of the piercing channel.
Post-Cleaning Care

Once your piercing is clean and dry, leave it alone as much as possible. Avoid irritants like sleeping on the piercing, touching or playing with it, submerging it in water, applying creams or makeup, and bumping or snagging the piercing.

The healing process begins immediately after your piercing. Here's what to expect at different stages:

1. Initial Reaction (1-2 Weeks)

  Symptoms: Inflammation, bleeding, bruising, and tenderness.

  Duration: These symptoms typically last 1-2 weeks but may take longer to subside.

2. Regeneration Phase (A Few Days to Several Weeks)

  Secretions: Expect some off-white or yellowish discharge (serous exudate) and crusting around the jewelry.

  Cleaning: Regular cleaning is crucial throughout this phase to manage the buildup of secretions.

3. Fistula Formation (Several Months)

  Scar Tissue: Your body builds a secure tube of skin (fistula) around the jewelry.

  Jewelry Stability: This process is why the angle of your piercing can't be adjusted after healing starts.

  Perceived Healing: Piercings heal from the outside in, making them appear healed sooner than they actually are.

  Check-Ups: We recommend regular check-ups to monitor progress or before changing jewelry.

Healing Timelines

These timelines are general guidelines; individual healing times may vary:

  • Soft Tissue Piercings: 3-6 months
  • Cartilage Piercings: 6-12 months
  • Nipple, Navel, Genital, and Dermal Piercings: 6-12+ months

It's normal to experience some level of discomfort or pain during a piercing procedure. Pain tolerance varies among individuals and can be influenced by attitude.

While it's natural to feel nervous beforehand, the sensation of a piercing is typically brief—a quick pinch or pressure that subsides quickly. Once you see your stunning new jewelry, any discomfort will likely be forgotten.
